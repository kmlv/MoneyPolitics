{% blocktrans trimmed %} Generating Initial Wealth {% endblocktrans %}

In every round, every participant will perform a task. After the task, the computer will generate a ranking of performance in the task for all nine participants. Your position in the ranking may or may not determine your initial wealth following a probabilistic rule.

There will be six wealth levels: 9, 15, 25, 40, 80, and 125. If your wealth is generated by your ranking the following table will apply:

Ranking in task 1st 2nd 3rd 4th 5th 6th 7th 8th 9th
Initial Wealth 125 points 80 points 40 points 25 points 25 points 15 points 15 points 15 points 9 points

Notice there are nine participants, but only six different wealth levels. Two participants, ranks 4 and 5, both have wealth level 25, and three participants, ranks 6, 7 and 8, each have wealth level 15.

After the effort task, your ranking will be determined, and the the computer will flip a virtual coin. If it comes up heads, all participants will have their initial wealth determined by their ranking in the task as shown in the table above. If it comes up tails, every participant's wealth will be randomly shuffled and income will be based completely on luck.

{% if tax_system == 'tax_rate' %}
{% blocktrans trimmed %} Selecting a Tax Rate for the Group {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Participants’ initial wealth levels help to determine their earnings in the experiment by serving as a potential resource that they may invest in an economic activity. Exactly how much each participant earns is also determined by (1) the amounts of public goods (analogous to roads, bridges, public transit, and other government services in the real world) that are funded by taxes paid by the participants, and (2) the amount of tax that each individual pays. In general, you’ll earn more if there are more public goods being funded by the tax payments of the group as a whole, and you’ll earn more if the amount of tax that you personally pay is lower, since paying more tax leaves you less to put into your own economic activity. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Once all participants have been informed of their initial wealth levels, the group will collectively decide the tax level that will be binding for all participants. Each person will vote for a tax rate that they would like to be applied to all participants, themselves included. Once the votes are submitted, the median of all submitted preferred tax rates will be determined and selected as the tax rate applicable to the group. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} How is the median determined? {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} In the context of nine values, the median M is defined as the value such that there are five numbers that are smaller than or equal to M; and there are five numbers that are greater than or equal to M. For example, suppose you and your friends must all choose an amount to contribute to pay for the food at a party. You select $5, and the other eight participants of your group have chosen: $1, $4, $8, $9, $10, $7, $8, $2. To find the median number, you can sort from low to high as in the table below, then find the number in the middle position (position 5). That is the median. {% endblocktrans %}

Number Order 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9
Contribution $2 $3 $3 $6 $6 $9 $10 $12 $16

{% blocktrans trimmed %} The median in the example would be $6, which is the middle amount in a sorted list. When you and other participants select a tax rate, the median amount will be computed in this fashion. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Payoffs {% endblocktrans %}
{% if msg_type == 'tax_rate' %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} The payoff for the round is calculated as follows. First, the total cost of the messages you sent is subtracted from your initial wealth. Then each participant's wealth is taxed at the chosen (median) tax rate. This generates two relevant values: what is left after taxes (a private resource) and the total amount of tax revenue (viewed as resources that fund public goods). {% endblocktrans %}

{% elif msg_type == 'progressivity' %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} The payoff for the round is calculated as follows. First, the total cost of the messages you sent is subtracted from your initial wealth. Then each participant's wealth is taxed by the respective tax according to the chosen (median) tax system. This generates two relevant values: what is left after taxes (a private resource) and the total amount of tax revenue (viewed as resources that fund public goods). {% endblocktrans %}

{% endif %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} The payoff takes into consideration the resources that have been paid to the government. In particular, we consider the direct benefit from public goods (ex.: the benefit of social security, or unemployment insurance) and the indirect benefit via positive effects of public goods on our own private productivity (ex.: the benefit that companies receive from having roads and private property rights). {% endblocktrans %}
