{% blocktrans trimmed %} Instructions {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} The following experiment is a public goods game in which you will be assigned to a group of nine participants. Together you will play the public goods game for X rounds - each round will have similar rules. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Income Generation {% endblocktrans %}

In every round, every group member will perform an income generating task. Your performance in the task will give you a ranking, and your ranking will determine your private income for the round. This conversion of ranking to income is explained in the table below:

Income Assignment by Skill 125 80 40 25 25 15 15 15 9
Ranking in Real Effort Task 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9

Notice there are nine members but only six different income levels, therefor some members will have the same income as other members. Please also note that income WILL NOT be determined solely by task performance, it will have a component of luck. There will be a random selection of two equally likely scenarios. The first of which is a situation where three player's incomes will be shuffled while six player's incomes will remain based solely on skill. The second situation has six player's incomes shuffled while three player's incomes stay based solely on skill. Simply, your income is generated based on your ranking in the effort task and then may be shuffled randomly with either three or six other members depending on a random selection.

Every message will be distributed after everyone has finished writing their messages. Messages are unidirectional and there will not be any back and forth communication. Please note that you may not send a message that contains inappropriate language or conveys a threat.

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Selecting Group Tax Rate {% endblocktrans %}

The group will then collectively decide the tax level in the group. Each person will vote for a tax rate that they would like to be applied to all members, themselves included. Once the votes are submitted, the median of the votes will be determined and selected as the tax rate applicable to all members of the group.

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Example of a median: Suppose you and your friends must all choose an amount to contribute for food at a party. If you select $5 and the other 8 members of your group have chosen: $1, $4, $8, $9, $10, $7, $8, $2. Sorted from low to high we have:

{% endblocktrans %} 1: $1
2: $2
3: $4
4: $5
5: $7 (= {% blocktrans trimmed %}median amount{% endblocktrans %})
6: $8
7: $8
8: $9
9: $10

{% blocktrans trimmed %} The median in the example would be $7. Which is the middle amount in a sorted list. When you and other members of your group select a tax rate, the median amount will be computed in this fashion. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Payoffs {% endblocktrans %}

Each members private income from the income generation task will be taxed at the group median determined tax rate. The total amount of points spent on the messaging (total number of messages times cost per message) will be subtracted from the private income as well. The payoff also takes into consideration the amount of money that has been received by the goverment though tax revenue in the following ways. First there is a direct benefit from public goods that is given to the player (Ex: the benefit of social security, or unemployment insurance). And second there will be a multiplier applied to the private payoffs that encompasses the positive effects of solid public goods on private productivity (Ex: the benefit that companies receive from having roads and private property rights).
